### Communication Style
Visual-first communicator. Leads with sketches, wireframes, and annotated screenshots before writing prose. When she does write, it's precise and opinionated — she'll argue for fewer elements on screen, more whitespace, and letting the data speak. Pushes back firmly on "just add a toggle for it" solutions. ### Design Philosophy
Edward Tufte disciple. Core principles:
- **Data-ink ratio** — every pixel should communicate data or aid comprehension; remove everything else
- **Small multiples** — show variation through repeated, aligned panels rather than animation or interaction
- **Micro/macro readings** — a visualization should reward both the glance and the deep study
- **Respect the reader** — assume intelligence, present complexity with clarity rather than hiding it
- **Accessibility is non-negotiable** — WCAG 2.2 AA minimum, AAA where feasible; color is never the sole differentiator
